## 3 · About (paste as one block)

```
I started asking buildings a question my software couldn't answer: how will this space make someone feel?

Chasing it turned me from an architect into a Design Technology Architect. Now I build the missing tools. NeuroSpace reads a floor plan and estimates what its defaults are doing to the mind (cortisol, circadian rhythm, cognitive load), live in the browser. Sensi, winner at the MaCAD Awards 2026, makes comfort a design layer: six senses, calibrated to a person, not an average. I call where this is going Behavior Information Modeling, and the podcast conversation where the term was born (with Dr. Cleo Valentine, Cambridge) is pinned below.

Before the tools came the practice: computational design for towers in Dubai (facade and massing studies in Rhino and Grasshopper), BIM delivery, exhibition design in Kuwait, a design project featured in the Biennale Arte 2024. Grasshopper and Kangaroo are my first languages. AI-assisted development is how I ship the rest: real, working software, this portfolio included.

Is any of it finished science? Not even close. But somebody has to teach our models that a facade is a public health decision, and I'd like to be first in line.

If your team is trying to make buildings answer harder questions, let's talk.
```

**Main draft:**

Comfort is usually the thing we hope shows up after the design is done.

Last month our studio project Sensi won the Design Copilots category at the MaCAD Awards 2026, at IAAC's Year 1 exhibition. There was a ceremony. There was a trophy. There was a team grinning like we'd just passed a crit we secretly loved.

Sensi is a copilot that reads a floor plan and scores it across six senses (thermal, visual, acoustic, spatial, olfactory, tactile), calibrated to a person, not an average. You don't walk into a room and average your experience: the thing that's wrong is the thing you notice. So we taught a copilot to notice it first.

The 45 seconds below show it doing exactly that.

Built with Lakzhmy Mari Zaro, María Sánchez Domínguez, and Charles Abi Chahine; guided by João M. Silva, Bao Trinh, and Scott Lebow at IAAC.
